## Decision one: a pure, deterministic session engine (ADR-0006)

{/*
  DEVELOP: Your flagship decision. Give it the most room.
  - The before: exam logic lived in a ~350-line hook, leaning on useRef to dodge
    stale closures and on module-level singletons. Slow to test (jest-expo renders
    hooks; the suite took ~10 minutes) and fragile on state reset.
  - The decision: extract the logic into a pure engine (src/engine/) with no React,
    Expo, or globals. Plain data in (GPS fixes, clock ticks, voice exchanges),
    plain data out (events, speech requests, state transitions). useDrivingSession.ts
    becomes a thin adapter to device APIs.
  - The payoff (the trade-off accepted): a large refactor cost, but it unlocks
    deterministic replay, a real GPS track plus a transcript replayed in a unit
    test in milliseconds, and it makes the later Android port (MVP-5) feasible by
    reusing the engine intact. The suite now runs in ~4s, down from ~10min.
  - Why this is the decision that best demonstrates judgement: it trades short-term
    effort for testability, portability, and confidence. That is engineering, not
    output.
*/}

## Decision two: real NZTA scoring, not an invented percentage (ADR-0005)

{/*
  DEVELOP: Your product-judgement decision, not just technical.
  - The choice: align scoring to official NZTA categories (critical errors,
    immediate-fail conditions) rather than a percentage score invented for effect.
  - Why it matters: it answers the user's real question, "would I have passed",
    instead of a number that looks precise but means nothing.
  - The trade-off accepted: it requires maintaining an event-to-category mapping
    table by hand, and the NZTA-aligned engine is still MVP-1 work, not yet
    complete. Be honest about that here.
  - CAVEAT to flag: alignment to NZTA categories is a design intent, not a claim of
    official equivalence or endorsement. Keep the wording careful.
*/}

## Decision three: the honest gap, hands-free audio (ADR-0003)

{/*
  DEVELOP: The decision that shows honesty and risk-thinking. Do not hide it.
  - The core product constraint: a truly hands-free session, phone untouched for
    the full twenty minutes. This is the vision.
  - The reality: the original voice library crashed on an AVAudioPCMBuffer
    exception and was replaced by expo-speech-recognition, but true always-listening
    is unresolved. Today it is tap-to-speak, which contradicts the central
    constraint. This is the largest gap between product vision and current code.
  - The framing: name it as an open spike (full-duplex vs half-duplex), a known
    unknown being worked deliberately, not a failure swept aside. MVP-2 is blocked
    on this spike, and saying so is a strength, not an apology.
*/}

## Other decisions worth noting

{/*
  DEVELOP (briefly, one short paragraph each or a tight prose cluster):
  - ADR-0001: all AI calls go through a Supabase Edge Function proxy. One extra
    network hop (tens of ms) in exchange for never shipping API keys in the client
    bundle, a blocker for TestFlight distribution.
  - ADR-0002: guest tier fully ephemeral, nothing persisted. Loses local-to-account
    progress migration, but avoids orphaned data and gives the simplest privacy
    story.
  - ADR-0004: OSM/Overpass as the source of speed limits and checkpoints, replacing
    a hardcoded 50 km/h limit and a sign-detection path that never fired. Costs an
    extra network call and imperfect NZ data coverage.
  - Positioning: MapView.onUserLocationChange as the authoritative source (not
    followsUserLocation, unreliable with PROVIDER_GOOGLE); watchPositionAsync as
    fallback.
  - Dynamic rerouteing: no fixed route; recalculated from current GPS whenever a
    leg completes or the driver strays >300m, with a 20s debounce.
*/}
